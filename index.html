Bank Performance Ratios

Interest Income %
Interest Income % = (Interest Earned ÷ Total Income) × 100
This ratio shows the proportion of total income earned from core banking activities like lending and investments. A high value indicates strong earning efficiency. A low value suggests reliance on non-core or uncertain income sources.
Net Interest Income % (NII%)
Net Interest Income % = (Net Interest Income ÷ Total Income) × 100
Measures the spread between interest earned and interest paid. High ratio shows effective asset-liability management, low ratio indicates narrow margins or high cost of funds.
Net Profit Margin % (NPM%)
Net Profit Margin % = (Net Profit ÷ Total Income) × 100
Reflects profitability per unit of income. High NPM indicates efficient cost control and strong earnings. Low NPM implies higher operating costs or weaker revenue generation.
Net Interest Margin (NIM%)
NIM % = (Net Interest Income ÷ (Advances + Investments)) × 100
Indicates how efficiently the bank’s earning assets generate profit. High NIM means better returns, low NIM indicates poor yield or rising interest costs.
Total Business
Total Business = Total Deposits + Total Advances
Shows the overall operational size of the bank. High total business indicates strong growth and market presence. Low value may suggest limited lending or deposit mobilization.
CASA Ratio
CASA Ratio = ((Demand Deposits + Savings Deposits) ÷ Total Deposits) × 100
Measures the proportion of low-cost deposits. High CASA ratio indicates cheap and stable funding. Low CASA ratio shows dependence on expensive term deposits.
Loans to Deposits Ratio (LDR)
LDR = (Total Advances ÷ Total Deposits) × 100
Indicates how well the bank deploys its deposits into loans. High LDR shows efficient fund utilization, but if too high, it may pose liquidity risks. Low LDR implies underutilized deposits.
Capital Adequacy Ratio (CAR)
CAR = ((Capital + Reserves) ÷ Risk-Weighted Assets) × 100
Measures the bank’s capital strength against risks. High CAR reflects strong solvency and depositor safety. Low CAR (below regulatory minimum, e.g., 9%) indicates weak capital buffer and higher financial risk.
Return on Investment (ROI)
ROI = (Net Profit ÷ Total Investment) × 100
Assesses the profitability of investments. High ROI indicates effective use of capital, while low ROI may signal inefficiencies.
Return on Equity (ROE)
ROE = (Net Income ÷ Shareholder's Equity) × 100
Evaluates the profitability relative to equity. High ROE signifies effective management and strong financial performance, while low ROE may indicate inefficiencies.
Return on Assets (ROA)
ROA = (Net Profit ÷ Total Assets) × 100
Assesses how efficiently a bank utilizes its assets to generate profit. High ROA indicates effective asset management, while low ROA may signal inefficiencies.
Return on Fixed Assets (ROFA)
ROFA = (Net Profit ÷ Fixed Assets) × 100
Evaluates the profitability generated from fixed assets. High ROFA indicates effective use of fixed assets, while low ROFA may suggest inefficiencies.
Return on Capital Employed (ROCE)
ROCE = (EBIT ÷ Capital Employed) × 100
Measures the profitability and efficiency of capital use. High ROCE indicates effective capital utilization, while low ROCE may suggest inefficiencies.
No. of Equity Shares

No. of Equity Shares = Total Equity ÷ Face Value per Share
Indicates the total number of shares issued by the bank. High number of shares may suggest extensive capital raising, while low number of shares may indicate a more conservative approach.
Earning Per Share (EPS)

EPS = ((Net Income - Preference Shares Dividends) ÷ No. of Equity Shares)
Indicates the portion of a bank's profit allocated to each outstanding share. High EPS suggests better profitability, while low EPS may indicate challenges.
Price to Earnings (P/E) Ratio

P/E Ratio = (Market Price per Share ÷ Earnings Per Share)
Indicates the market's expectations of a company's future financial performance. High P/E Ratio suggests strong growth potential, while low P/E Ratio may indicate undervaluation or challenges.
Dividend Payout Ratio

Dividend Payout Ratio = (Dividends Paid Per Share ÷ Earnings Per Share) × 100
Indicates the proportion of earnings distributed to shareholders as dividends. High Dividend Payout Ratio may suggest a stable income for investors, while low Dividend Payout Ratio may indicate reinvestment in growth.
Dividend Yield
Dividend Yield = (Dividends Paid per Share ÷ Market Price per Share) × 100
Indicates the return on investment for shareholders in the form of dividends. High Dividend Yield may attract income-focused investors, while low Dividend Yield may suggest reinvestment in growth.
